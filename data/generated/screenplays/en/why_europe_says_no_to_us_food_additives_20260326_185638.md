# SCREENPLAY: European Food Additive Bans vs. US Standards

## 

**TITLE:** Why Europe Says 'No' To US Food Additives

### [0-2s] HOOK
**VISUAL:** Extreme close-up of creator's face, looking directly at camera with raised eyebrow, kitchen background slightly blurred
**VOICEOVER:** "Did you know these additives are banned in Europe?" (shocked, conspiratorial tone)
**ON-SCREEN TEXT:** "BANNED IN EUROPE ❌🇪🇺"
**SOUND/MUSIC:** Sharp attention-grabbing sound effect, upbeat investigative music begins

### [2-10s] SETUP
**VISUAL:** Quick montage - hands grabbing various US food packages from pantry shelves, laying them out on marble countertop. Close-up shots of ingredient labels.
**VOICEOVER:** "While the US approved over 3,000 food additives, Europe has banned hundreds for health risks. Let me show you what's hiding in your pantry." (educational, slightly concerned tone)
**ON-SCREEN TEXT:** "US: 3,000+ additives approved" / "EU: Hundreds banned for health risks"
**SOUND/MUSIC:** Investigative music continues, package rustling sounds

### [10-45s] CORE
**VISUAL:** 
- [10-15s] Close-up of Mountain Dew bottle, finger pointing to "Yellow 5" on label
- [15-20s] Close-up of Wonder Bread package, highlighting "Azodicarbonamide"  
- [20-25s] Close-up of Kraft Mac & Cheese box, pointing to "Yellow 6"
- [25-30s] Close-up of Hostess Twinkies package, showing multiple additives
- [30-35s] Split screen: US products on left, European equivalent products on right
- [35-40s] Creator holding up both products, comparing labels
- [40-45s] Close-up of creator's concerned expression

**VOICEOVER:** "Mountain Dew contains Yellow 5 - banned in Norway and Austria due to hyperactivity links. Wonder Bread uses azodicarbonamide, the same chemical in yoga mats, banned across the EU since 2005. Kraft Mac & Cheese has Yellow 6, restricted in the UK for requiring warning labels. And Hostess Twinkies? They contain BHT, banned in Japan and the EU as a potential carcinogen. Meanwhile, European versions use natural alternatives like turmeric and paprika extract." (informative, building concern)

**ON-SCREEN TEXT:** 
- "Yellow 5 = Banned in Norway & Austria"
- "Azodicarbonamide = In your bread & yoga mats!"
- "Yellow 6 = Warning labels required in UK"
- "BHT = Banned in EU & Japan"

### [45-50s] CTA
**VISUAL:** Creator leaning toward camera, holding up a European food package and US equivalent
**VOICEOVER:** "Since 1960, US adult obesity jumped from 13.4% to 43% in 2024. Coincidence? Start reading those labels, mi amor." (empowering, direct tone)
**ON-SCREEN TEXT:** "1960: 13.4% obesity → 2024: 43% obesity"
**SOUND/MUSIC:** Music builds to conclusion, confident tone
