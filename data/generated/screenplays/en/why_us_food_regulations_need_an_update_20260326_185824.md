## **SCREENPLAY: "Why US Food Regulations Need an Update!"**

### **[0-2s] HOOK**
**VISUAL:** Close-up of creator's face, serious expression, kitchen background slightly blurred. Camera slowly zooms out.
**VOICEOVER:** (Urgent, concerned tone) "US food laws are stuck in the past. Here's why it matters..."
**ON-SCREEN TEXT:** "1938 Food Laws Still Rule Today 😱"
**SOUND/MUSIC:** Dramatic pause, then upbeat educational music begins

### **[2-10s] SETUP**
**VISUAL:** Quick montage of black-and-white footage style shots: old-fashioned kitchen, vintage food packaging, then cut to modern processed food aisle in supermarket. Creator walks into frame holding a piece of chalk.
**VOICEOVER:** (Educational, slightly dramatic) "The Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act of 1938 — that's 86 years ago — still governs what we eat today. But our food system has completely transformed."
**ON-SCREEN TEXT:** "1938 vs 2024" with split screen effect
**SOUND/MUSIC:** Vintage-style music transitioning to modern beat

### **[10-35s] CORE**
**VISUAL:** Creator stands in front of a large chalkboard, writing key statistics with chalk. Cut between close-ups of her hands writing and medium shots showing the full board. Fresh fruits and vegetables arranged on counter nearby.

**VOICEOVER:** (Passionate, informative) "Look at these obesity rates from the CDC: In 1960, only 13.4% of adults and 5% of children were obese. By 2024? A staggering 43% of adults and 21% of children. That's tripled for adults, quadrupled for kids!"

**VISUAL:** Camera zooms in on chalkboard showing:
- 1960: Adults 13.4%, Kids 5%
- 2024: Adults 43%, Kids 21%

**VOICEOVER CONTINUES:** (Emphasizing each point) "Meanwhile, the 1938 Act required safety evidence for new drugs, but food additives? Still loosely regulated. We're using 86-year-old rules for a completely different food landscape!"

**ON-SCREEN TEXT:** "Same Laws, Different World"
**SOUND/MUSIC:** Steady educational beat with emphasis hits on statistics

### **[35-45s] CTA**
**VISUAL:** Creator puts down chalk, turns to camera with fresh produce in hands. Close-up on her face, then pulls back to show colorful array of fruits and vegetables.
**VOICEOVER:** (Empowering, direct) "Until these laws catch up, we have to protect ourselves. Choose whole foods, read every ingredient, and demand better from our representatives!"
**ON-SCREEN TEXT:** "Your Health = Your Choice 💪"
**SOUND/MUSIC:** Uplifting, motivational music builds to climax

## **PRODUCTION DETAILS**

**PROPS NEEDED:**
- Large chalkboard or blackboard
- White chalk
- Fresh produce variety: apples, oranges, leafy greens, tomatoes, bell peppers
- Vintage-style kitchen props (optional for b-roll)
- Modern processed food packages (for contrast shots)

**SHOOTING NOTES:**
- Use warm kitchen lighting for modern shots, cooler/dimmer for "vintage" aesthetic
- Chalkboard should be well-lit to ensure text is readable
- Get close-up shots of hand writing statistics for dynamic editing
- Use shallow depth of field to make produce pop against background
- Consider split-screen effects in post for 1938 vs 2024 comparison

**HASHTAGS:** 
#FoodRegulation #HealthyEating #FoodPolicy #Nutrition #HealthyLifestyle #FoodSafety #WellnessEducation #HealthTips

**MUSIC:** 
Trending educational/documentary style beat that builds from serious to empowering. Consider sounds that evoke both vintage (for historical context) and modern (for current relevance).
