# GRAS: THE LOOPHOLE IN YOUR FOOD



**PROPS NEEDED:**
- Various packaged foods with long ingredient lists (Twinkies, Doritos, instant mac & cheese)
- Red marker
- White poster board
- Calculator
- Ring light for close-ups
- Smartphone with timer

**SHOOTING NOTES:**
- Use macro lens for extreme close-ups of ingredient labels
- Shoot against white background for clean aesthetic
- Ensure good lighting for text readability
- Film reaction shots with shallow depth of field
- Use quick cuts to maintain energy

---

**VISUAL:** Extreme close-up of creator's shocked face, eyes wide, holding a Hostess Twinkie package. Camera slowly pulls back to reveal her in kitchen.

**VOICEOVER:** (Dramatic whisper) "Did you know companies can add new chemicals to food without telling the FDA?"

**ON-SCREEN TEXT:** "WITHOUT TELLING THE FDA?!"

**SOUND:** Suspenseful sting, then upbeat cooking music fades in

**[2-10s] SETUP**

**VISUAL:** Creator flips Twinkie package to show ingredient list. Extreme close-up of ingredients as she circles them with red marker.

**VOICEOVER:** (Conversational, slightly outraged) "I'm reading this Twinkie label and I see polysorbate 60, sodium stearoyl lactylate, calcium sulfate... Girl, what IS this stuff?"

**ON-SCREEN TEXT:** Ingredients highlighted: "POLYSORBATE 60" "SODIUM STEAROYL LACTYLATE"

**SOUND:** Marker squeaking on package

**[10-45s] CORE**

**VISUAL:** Cut to creator at counter with poster board. She writes "GRAS" in large letters.

**VOICEOVER:** (Educational but animated) "It's all legal thanks to something called GRAS — Generally Recognized As Safe. In 1996, the FDA formalized a process where companies can basically police themselves."

**ON-SCREEN TEXT:** "GRAS = Generally Recognized As Safe" / "1996: Self-Policing Begins"

**VISUAL:** Creator holds up calculator, starts pressing buttons dramatically.

**VOICEOVER:** (Building intensity) "Check this out — in 1996, adult obesity was 22.5%. By 2024? It hit 43%! That's nearly DOUBLED since companies started adding mystery chemicals without FDA approval."

**ON-SCREEN TEXT:** Split screen showing "1996: 22.5%" vs "2024: 43.0%"

**VISUAL:** Side-by-side comparison - creator holds homemade cookie in one hand, packaged cookie in other.

**VOICEOVER:** (Passionate) "My homemade cookies? Flour, butter, sugar, eggs, vanilla — five ingredients. This Chips Ahoy package? Thirty-seven ingredients including high fructose corn syrup, soy lecithin, and artificial flavors that never had to be proven safe!"

**ON-SCREEN TEXT:** "HOMEMADE: 5 ingredients" vs "CHIPS AHOY: 37 ingredients"

**[45-60s] CTA**

**VISUAL:** Creator leans into camera, holding up both packages with serious expression.

**VOICEOVER:** (Direct, empowering) "Companies bet you won't read labels. Prove them wrong. If you can't pronounce it, research it. Your body deserves better than their chemistry experiment."

**ON-SCREEN TEXT:** "READ YOUR LABELS" / "YOU DESERVE BETTER"

**VISUAL:** Creator tosses processed package in trash, takes bite of homemade cookie, winks.

**SOUND:** Satisfying crunch sound effect
