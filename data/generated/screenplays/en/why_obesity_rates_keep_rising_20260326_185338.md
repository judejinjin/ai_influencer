# OBESITY RATES RISING SCREENPLAY

## 

### SCREENPLAY STRUCTURE

**[0-2s] HOOK**
- **VISUAL:** Close-up of creator's face, serious expression, holding up a vintage food package from the 1960s in one hand and modern processed food in the other
- **VOICEOVER:** "Spot the trend with me—obesity rates are skyrocketing!" (urgent, concerned tone)
- **ON-SCREEN TEXT:** "1960 vs 2024" with arrow pointing up
- **SOUND/MUSIC:** Dramatic sting, then upbeat investigative music

**[2-10s] SETUP**
- **VISUAL:** Quick montage: Creator pointing to printed graphs on kitchen counter, finger tracing upward trend line. Cut to her arranging vintage vs. modern food packages side by side
- **VOICEOVER:** "In 1960, only 13.4% of American adults were obese. By 2024? We've hit 43%! That's triple the rate in just sixty years." (shocked, emphasizing numbers)
- **ON-SCREEN TEXT:** "1960: 13.4%" → "2024: 43.0%" with dramatic red arrow
- **SOUND/MUSIC:** Continued investigative beat with data-reveal sound effects

**[10-35s] CORE**
- **VISUAL:** Creator holds up meat package, points to ingredient list. Cut to split-screen showing US meat label vs. European equivalent. Then shows timeline graphic of deregulation milestones overlaid on fast-food imagery
- **VOICEOVER:** "Here's what they don't want you to know—the US allows Ractopamine in our pork, beef, and turkey as a growth promoter. This chemical is banned in the European Union, China, Russia, and over 160 countries because of cardiovascular concerns. But here? Totally legal." (investigative, building intensity)
- **ON-SCREEN TEXT:** "RACTOPAMINE: BANNED IN 160+ COUNTRIES" / "LEGAL IN USA"
- **SOUND/MUSIC:** Tension building, dramatic reveal beats

**[35-45s] CTA**
- **VISUAL:** Creator looking directly at camera, holding up both food packages again, then dramatically tosses the processed one aside
- **VOICEOVER:** "When profit comes before people, this is what happens. Check your labels, demand better, and let's change this trend together!" (empowered, call-to-action tone)
- **ON-SCREEN TEXT:** "READ YOUR LABELS" / "DEMAND BETTER"
- **SOUND/MUSIC:** Empowering, uplifting crescendo

### PROPS NEEDED:
- Printed obesity rate graphs (1960-2024)
- Vintage food packaging (1960s-70s era)
- Modern processed meat packages
- European food labels for comparison
- Timeline graphic of US food deregulation
- Kitchen counter/clean background setup

### SHOOTING NOTES:
- Use macro lens for close-up label details
- Ensure good lighting on graphs/text for readability
- Quick cuts between statistics for dynamic pacing
- Keep creator centered, use hand gestures to emphasize points
- Film split-screen comparisons with consistent lighting
